AI System Design | A13104

Classifying Air Pollution
Risk Levels in Makkah

A Comprehensive Explainable AI (XAI) framework to support decision-making and improve urban quality of life.

Linah Alsayed(Leader) Danah Alharbi Salam Alghamdi Khadija Mirza
REPORT PHASE 1 | SECTION 1

Problem Statement & Context

The Context & Challenge
  • With urban expansion, environmental pollution levels have increased significantly.
  • Air pollution has become one of the most significant challenges affecting quality of life and public health.
  • Data collected from Makkah indicates varying percentages of households exposed to different pollution types.
The Gap & Solution
  • The problem lies in the absence of a system that analyzes this data, classifies risk levels, and ranks types by impact.
  • This makes it difficult for authorities to determine priority areas for intervention.
  • Goal: Develop a system to classify risks, support decision-making, and identify priority actions.
REPORT PHASE 1 | SECTIONS 2 & 3

Objectives & Full Requirements

4 Main Objectives
  1. Classify air pollution types based on risk level.
  2. Rank pollution types according to priority.
  3. Provide data-driven recommendations for authorities.
  4. Contribute to improving quality of life and public health.
System Requirements

Functional: Input percentage data, Preprocessing/cleaning, Risk classification (Low, Med, High), Ranking pollution, Visualizing results (tables/charts), Providing recommendations.

Non-Functional: Reliable accuracy, Clear outputs, Ability to update/extend to other cities, Fast processing, Simple/user-friendly design.

Research & Public
  • Researchers & Analysts: Analyze pollution patterns and develop further predictive models.

REPORT PHASE 1 | SECTION 5

All 7 Success Metrics & KPIs

Model Performance
  • Accuracy: Target ≥ 80% for correct classification.
  • Precision & Recall: Target ≥ 75% for reliable high-risk identification.
  • F1-Score: Target ≥ 0.75 (balanced measure).
  • Ranking Effectiveness: Consistency between ranking and actual impact severity.
System Usability
  • Processing Time: Results generated within a few seconds.
  • Usability & Clarity: Outputs easily interpretable by non-technical users.
  • Scalability: Maintain performance with extended datasets/cities.

REPORT PHASE 1 | SECTIONS 7.1 & 7.2

Complete Ethical & Legal Considerations

Ethical (7.1)
  • Transparency & Interpretability: Selected Decision Tree to avoid "black-box" models. Explains exactly *why* a risk is high.
  • Fairness: Must ensure system does not unfairly bias against peripheral neighborhoods lacking sensors compared to central areas.
Legal (7.2)
  • Data Protection: Strict compliance with Saudi PDPL ensuring complete anonymization of demographic data.
  • Liability & Disclaimer: System is a DSS. Development team bears no legal responsibility for urban planning decisions. AI provides probabilistic indicators, not facts.
REPORT PHASE 1 | SECTIONS 7.3, 7.4, 7.5

Societal, Assumptions & Constraints

Societal (7.3) & Assumptions (7.4)
  • Real Estate: Labeling areas "hazardous" could induce panic and affect rental prices. Outputs are for policymakers only.
  • Quality of Life: Positively enables resource allocation to affected areas.
  • Assumptions: Data is accurate/up-to-date; primary patterns are known with no unrecorded disasters.
Constraints (7.5)
  • Seasonality: Makkah's unique surges (Hajj/Umrah) make it hard to distinguish permanent vs. temporary baseline pollution.
  • Missing Data: Difficult to acquire dense data for every single neighborhood, limiting accuracy in under-monitored areas.
REPORT PHASE 1 | SECTIONS 8.1 - 8.4

UCD, XAI & Governance

Mutual Benefit & XAI (8.1, 8.2)
  • Benefit Loop: Providers (Gov/households) supply raw data → Receivers (Authorities) get structured insights.
  • XAI: Decision tree chosen for transparency. Authorities can trace if risk is permanent (waste) or temporary (Hajj).
Ethical AI & Governance (8.3, 8.4)
  • Fairness & Objective Analysis: Thresholds documented consistently. Grounded in data, minimizing human bias.
  • Privacy Compliance: PDPL adherence via aggregated stats.

REPORT PHASE 2 | IDENTIFICATION & JUSTIFICATION

Full Data Source Justification

DataSaudi & GASTAT
  • Credibility & Trust: Since it's a DSS, we cannot use scraped/unofficial data. We need verified government platforms trusted by policymakers.
  • Targeted Relevance: IoT sensors aren't uniform in Makkah. DataSaudi provides actual household exposure % for 2024 (Dust 13.3%, Smoke 7.6%, etc.).
Algorithm & Privacy Fit
  • Algorithmic Fit: Limited temporal coverage suits our cross-sectional Decision Tree, rather than complex time-series.
  • Zero Privacy Risks: Data is already aggregated and anonymized. No PII is handled, ensuring immediate PDPL compliance.
REPORT PHASE 2 | COLLECTION & CLEANING

Collection & All Cleaning Steps

Collection Steps
  1. Access Household Environment Stats and filter for Makkah.
  2. Download 2024 dataset in CSV to prevent transcription errors.
  3. Load into pandas DataFrame.
3 Cleaning Areas
  • Missing Values: Given the small size and highly verified nature of the dataset, applying imputation could introduce bias. Therefore, we use listwise deletion (dropna()) to ensure the model learns only from 100% authentic records.
  • Format: Normalize to decimal (13.3% → 0.133), lowercase column names.
  • Outliers: Validate 0-100% range. Generate data quality report.
REPORT PHASE 2 | SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

Logical System Architecture

Workflow & Components

Local Storage (Excel) → Processing Layer (CLI) → ML Model (Decision Tree) → Results

  • Command-Line Interface (CLI): Provides a simple, lightweight environment for execution.
  • Data Processing Layer: Handles cleaning and preparing data directly from local Excel files, eliminating the need for a complex database.
  • ML Model (Decision Tree): Responsible for analyzing features and classifying risk levels into distinct categories.
  • Results & Visualization: Outputs confusion matrices, classification reports, and tree visualizations directly to the user.
REPORT PHASE 2 | SYSTEM ATTRIBUTES

Scalability, Fault-Tolerance & Security

Scalability & Reliability
  • Scalability: Highly modular. Python/pandas pipeline is not hardcoded to Makkah. Can scale to Riyadh/Jeddah without rewrite.
  • Reliability: Deterministic model. Same inputs yield same results, improving trust for policymakers.
Fault-Tolerance & Security
  • Fault-Tolerance: Python try-except blocks are utilized. The pipeline catches data loading errors, drops invalid rows smoothly, and continues execution without crashing the main application.
  • Security: PDPL compliance (anonymized data). Hosted locally or on internal gov infra to reduce cyber threats.
REPORT PHASE 2 | BASELINE & METRICS

Baseline Choice & All Metrics

Baseline: Decision Tree
  • Simple, understandable, and highly interpretable. Avoids "black-box" scenarios for government use.
  • Well-suited for small, structured datasets (numerical %). Uses simple threshold rules.
Evaluation Metrics
  • Accuracy: Overall correct classification.
  • Precision/Recall & F1: Vital for critical high-risk cases.
  • Risk Classification Logic: Because our system focuses on classifying risk levels rather than ranking, we ensure the model correctly categorizes severe pollution into the "High" risk class without false negatives.
REPORT PHASE 2 | EXPERIMENTATION

Complete Iterative Plan

The 3 Phases & Documentation
  • Iteration 1 (Baseline & Features): Transform continuous percentages into categorical risk labels (Low, Medium, High). Train a baseline Decision Tree to extract initial logical rules.
  • Iteration 2 (LOOCV Implementation): Instead of a standard train/test split, we implement the Leave-One-Out Cross-Validation (LOOCV) algorithm. This is the most mathematically rigorous approach for small datasets to prevent overfitting.
  • Iteration 3 (Metric Optimization): Refine the evaluation pipeline to handle zero-division edge cases, ensuring balanced performance metrics across all risk categories without artificial data inflation (e.g., SMOTE).
